# Two spines, and the wilderness survey is the higher-value half

**What it is.** From the import brief ([[import-design-brief]]): run **two** classification
spines over the corpus on one embedding pass —
- **Spine A — supervised routing:** match material to campaigns you *have*
  ([[0109-knn-not-centroids-drift]]).
- **Spine B — unsupervised survey:** cluster the *un-routed remainder* with no labels. Out
  fall abandoned worldbuilding seams, forgotten campaigns, magic-system tinkering scattered
  across unrelated chats, the meme thread.

The claim worth keeping: **Spine B is "arguably the higher-value half."** The campaign
sessions mostly already live in `solorpg`; the *floating seeds are genuinely lost*. And the
semantic index that makes this work is a **standing capability, not a one-time import
step** — concept retrieval over years of output ("every time I sketched a prison-dungeon
dynamic," "anything with portable-consciousness before Pale Horizon").

**Where it comes from.** `../solorpg/imports/IMPORT-DESIGN-BRIEF.md`, "Spine B" + "Bonus
capability." The user's own pre-planning.

**Why it matters for next-gen.** This is the **measured, mechanized answer** to
[[0064-unharvested-archive]] (the lost substrate) and the engine for
[[0065-oneshots-as-spawning-pool]] (floating seeds that graduate). It reframes "import"
from a migration chore into a recurring **idea-recovery tool** — which is a *product*, not
a script ([[0112-design-now-build-into-frame]]). The unsupervised half is where new
campaigns and reusable seams are *discovered*, not just sorted.

**Open threads.** Is wilderness-survey a one-time sweep or a standing tool re-run per dump
(an explicit open decision in the brief)? How do discovered `cluster` labels graduate into
real campaigns/themes (mirrors this vault's own tag→theme graduation)? Relationship to the
provenance graph ([[0111-provenance-graph-as-secret-history]]) — clusters are candidate
nodes in the secret history.

**Verdict.** _(unevaluated.)_
