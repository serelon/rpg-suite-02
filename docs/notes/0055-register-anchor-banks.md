# Register anchor banks — texture bank & living dictionary keep the *world's voice* in register

**What it is.** Two per-campaign reference artifacts that anchor a world's *register* rather
than its facts:
- **Texture bank** (`rust-and-ruin/reference/texture-bank.md`) — "the specific sensory and
  behavioral details that keep this world in register. Not plot, not character sheets."
  Organized by domain (The Workshop, The Camp, Bodies & Authority, Combat & Violence,
  Silence & Deflection) **and by POV register** ("The Outsider's Camp — Solace's register").
  Entries are largely **verbatim quotes harvested from play**, with sources tagged by
  session ("Sources: s01, s04, s05").
- **Living dictionary** (`lumina-city/reference/street-lingo.md`) — pidgin street vocabulary
  with etymologies and *generative rules*, not just a glossary: "words layer meanings —
  context decides… one word stretches to do new work, and the old meanings don't leave."

**Where it comes from.** User pointed at both during light workshop (2026-06-03); located
and read. The texture bank is what the user had remembered as a "style palette."

**Why it matters for next-gen.** Several threads converge here:
- This is the **sample-book grid living in production** ([[0006-sample-book-grid]],
  [[0005-exemplars-over-instructions]]) — exemplars organized by domain × register,
  **harvested from play** ([[0007-harvest-vs-workshop]]'s harvest arm, actually working).
- It's where **verbatim capture survived** — word-for-word clips with session provenance,
  exactly what the memory postprocessor lost ([[0054-verbatim-capture-lost-intent]]).
- It answers [[0053-anchor-hierarchy-voice-keystone]]'s open question: the keystone anchor
  for *world* drift is the same as for character drift — demonstrated voice. Characters get
  voice samples; worlds get texture banks and living dictionaries.
- The dictionary's generative-rule framing means it teaches the model to *coin* in-register
  terms, not just reuse listed ones — exemplar + rule, compressed.

**Provenance (user, 2026-06-03).** The texture bank was **extracted from sessions in play**;
the street-lingo file was **workshopped**. One artifact from each arm of
[[0007-harvest-vs-workshop]] — harvest and workshop both produce register anchors, and both
work. (Lingo is the "hard cell" you hand-seed; texture is the "easy cell" you clip from play —
matching 0007's bootstrapping-deadlock prediction.)
