# Oneshots as spawning pool — cheap, mostly fizzle, sometimes graduate three ways

**What it is.** Oneshots are a large share of actual play and the ecosystem's incubation
tier. Most "play through the end and that's it" — they fizzle, by design and at no cost.
But the survivors graduate, along at least three distinct paths (user, 2026-06-04):
- **→ campaign** ("several campaigns started as oneshots")
- **→ branch** of an existing campaign
- **→ story arc / canon timeskip** *inside* a campaign's continuity

**Where it comes from.** Live practice across the Desktop archive
([[0064-unharvested-archive]] — oneshots are a big slice of the ~500 unharvested threads).

**Why it matters for next-gen.** It's [[0043-campaigns-as-testbeds]]'s graduation dynamic
at the *content* level: low-cost experiments, high fizzle rate, occasional promotion. The
data model should support that lifecycle instead of ignoring it — a oneshot needs:
1. **A cheap home** — near-zero ceremony to start (one word can be the seed,
   [[0046-campaign-lifecycle-geological-strata]]); a full campaign folder is way too heavy.
2. **Graduation paths** — promotion to campaign/branch/arc should carry its material along
   (texture, characters, the transcript itself) rather than restarting from scratch.
3. **A dignified fizzle** — ended oneshots still hold harvestable texture and voice
   ([[0055-register-anchor-banks]]) even when their story is done.

The bundle compiler angle: a oneshot is arguably the *minimal compile target* — a fat,
self-contained, away-playable artifact ([[0063-portable-bundles-constraint]]) with no
persistent home required until it earns one.

**Open threads.** What triggers graduation in practice — the user just *knowing*, or could
debrief ask "does this want to continue?" Does a graduated oneshot's session become
session 01 retroactively (the campaign-setup skill's exact input)? Are canon-timeskip
oneshots a *fourth* axis for [[0047-multi-axis-data-management]] (per-era)?

**Verdict.** _(unevaluated.)_
